Project Location

Nilad Vaccination Site is located in San Andres, Manila City. We chose this location because we consider Manila City as a centralized city. It is known for its wide arrays of activities whether it is economic, political, leisure, or cultural-related.

San Andres is also the most densely populated district in Manila City and as of 2015, there is a record of 128, 499 residents living in the area.


Design Concept

The design concept used in this project is based on the Nilad Plant.

Nilad is a type of plant that thrived in the marshy conditions of Manila, particularly outside Intrumuros. It thrived because it was able to adapt to its environment.

Similar to this plant, we encorporate this trait to the idea behind this project. The idea that we may be in a marshy situation right now, but just like Nilad we will be able to adapt and thrive. Moreover, Nilad is the origin for the word, "Manila". Using this is the main project name makes the users feel rooted to their origin.


Design Philosophy

The philosophy used for this project is based on symbolism. We want this project to symbolize hope for the people in its community. Just like Nilad plant, we want our structure to symbolize our ability to adapt in a situation against our odds.

May this structure also remind the community that we can go on despite of our current situation, and through the vaccines offered in this vaccination center we will thrive once again.
